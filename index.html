Bio

I am an economist with research interests in demographic economics, development economics, and preference formation. My work uses quasi-experimental methods to study how institutions and economic shocks shape individual outcomes.

My primary research examines how socialist education in East Germany affected fertility outcomes after reunification. Using German reunification and school entry cutoffs as natural experiments, I show that additional years of socialist education affected completed fertility through the transmission of gender-egalitarian norms.

I work extensively with German administrative datasets (SOEP, NEPS, German Microcensus) and employ difference-in-differences, regression discontinuity designs, and other quasi-experimental identification strategies.

Working Papers

Socialist Education and Fertility: Evidence from German Reunification

Job Market Paper

PDF Abstract
This paper examines how socialist education in East Germany affected fertility outcomes after reunification. Using German reunification and school entry cutoffs as natural experiments, I show that additional years of socialist education reduced completed fertility. The mechanism operates through the transmission of gender-egalitarian norms: women exposed to more socialist education hold more egalitarian attitudes and are more likely to participate in the labor market, which in turn affects fertility decisions.

Aggregate Shocks During Adolescence and the Formation of Patience

(with [Coauthors])

Draft coming soon

Abstract
How do economic shocks during childhood and adolescence affect the formation of time preferences? Using cross-country data from 76 countries and 80,000 individuals, we examine how exposure to natural disasters, hyperinflation, and sovereign defaults during critical developmental windows affects long-term patience. We find that individuals who experienced major economic disruptions during adolescence exhibit systematically different time preferences in adulthood, with implications for savings behavior and human capital investment.
